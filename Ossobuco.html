Ossobuco

Ossobuco, a quintessential dish hailing from the Lombardy region of Italy, is a gastronomic masterpiece that showcases the sublime marriage of tender braised veal shanks with a rich and flavorful sauce. The name "Ossobuco" translates to "bone with a hole," a reference to the marrow-filled bone at the center of the succulent veal shank. This iconic Milanese dish is a celebration of slow-cooking, resulting in meat that effortlessly falls off the bone, paired with a luscious, aromatic sauce that will transport you straight to the heart of Italy.

Recipe for Traditional Fettuccine Alfredo

Ingredients

    1. For Ossobuco

    • 4 veal shanks, each about 2 inches thick
    • 1 cup all-purpose flour, for dredging
    • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
    • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
    • 4 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
    • 1 cup finely chopped onions
    • 1/2 cup finely chopped carrots
    • 1/2 cup finely chopped celery
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 cup dry white wine
    • 1 cup beef or veal stock
    • 1 can (14 ounces) crushed tomatoes
    • 2 bay leaves
    • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
    • Gremolata (optional, for garnish): Chopped fresh parsley, grated lemon zest, and minced garlic

Instructions

01

Prepare the Veal Shanks:

  • Pat the veal shanks dry with paper towels.
  • Dredge the shanks in flour, shaking off any excess.
  • Season the shanks generously with salt and pepper.

02

Sear the Veal Shanks:

  • In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat the butter and olive oil over medium-high heat.
  • Brown the veal shanks on all sides until golden and crusty.
  • Transfer the shanks to a plate.

03

Sauté the Aromatics:

  • In the same pot, add the onions, carrots, celery, and garlic.
  • Sauté until the vegetables are softened and aromatic.

04

Deglaze with Wine:

  • Pour in the white wine, scraping the browned bits from the bottom of the pot.
  • Allow the wine to reduce by half.

05

Braise the Veal:

  • Return the veal shanks to the pot.
  • Add the stock, crushed tomatoes, bay leaves, and thyme.
  • Bring the liquid to a simmer, then cover and reduce heat to low.
  • Let it simmer gently for about 2 hours or until the veal is fork-tender.

06

Finish and Serve:

  • Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  • Remove the bay leaves and discard.
  • Serve the ossobuco over a bed of risotto, polenta, or mashed potatoes.
  • Garnish with gremolata for a burst of freshness.

Delight in the symphony of flavors as you savor the velvety veal, enriched by the robust and aromatic sauce. Ossobuco alla Milanese is a dish that captures the essence of Italian cuisine, inviting you to experience the warmth and tradition of Lombardy on your plate. Buon Appetito!
