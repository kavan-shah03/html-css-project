Carpaccio

Carpaccio is a renowned Italian appetizer that originates from Harry's Bar in Venice, Italy. This dish typically features thinly sliced raw meat or fish, often beef or tuna, that is delicately seasoned and garnished with flavorful accompaniments. Carpaccio is celebrated for its elegant presentation and the harmony of its simple yet vibrant flavors, making it a beloved delicacy among enthusiasts of Italian cuisine.

Recipe for Traditional Carpaccio

Ingredients

    1. For Carpaccio

    • 200 grams of high-quality beef tenderloin
    • 2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil
    • 1 tablespoon of freshly squeezed lemon juice
    • A handful of arugula leaves
    • 30 grams of Parmesan cheese, shaved
    • Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
    • 1 teaspoon of capers (optional)
    • Freshly cracked black peppercorns (optional)
    • 1 lemon, sliced into wedges (for garnish)

Instructions

01

Select the Meat or Fish:

  • When selecting your ingredients, opt for high-quality options such as fresh beef tenderloin, tuna, or swordfish, ensuring that they meet the highest standards of freshness and quality.
  • Prioritize the freshness of the meat or fish you choose, ensuring that it is at the peak of its freshness to guarantee the best possible flavors in your dish.
  • Ensure that the meat or fish is of the highest grade for optimum taste and texture.

02

Prepare the Meat or Fish:

  • Trim any excess fat or sinew from the meat or remove the skin from the fish.
  • Wrap the meat or fish in plastic wrap and chill it in the freezer for about 1-2 hours until it is firm but not frozen solid.

03

Slice and Arrange the Meat or Fish:

  • Start by using a sharp knife to delicately slice the chilled meat or fish thinly, with the goal of achieving slices that are nearly transparent. This process demands careful attention and a steady hand.
  • Be meticulous and deliberate to create uniformly thin slices that contribute to the dish's visual appeal.
  • Carefully place them in a single layer on a chilled serving plate. Ensuring that the plate is adequately chilled helps to maintain the freshness of the meat or fish.
  • Take care to slightly overlap the slices on the serving plate. By doing so, you can create an alluring visual effect that adds depth and dimension to the presentation.

04

Prepare the Dressing:

  • To begin, take a small bowl and gather the necessary ingredients, which include:
    • A portion of extra-virgin olive oil
    • Freshly squeezed lemon juice
    • A pinch of salt
    • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Once you have all the ingredients in the bowl, mix them thoroughly, ensuring that they blend together harmoniously to create a cohesive mixture.

05

Garnish the Carpaccio:

  • Begin by sprinkling a handful of fresh arugula leaves over the meat or fish slices, ensuring an even distribution
  • Next, delicately add a scattering of capers, strategically placing them to provide a delightful burst of briny and tangy notes that complement the other elements on the plate.
  • Lastly, generously incorporate shaved Parmesan cheese, allowing its rich and nutty undertones to intertwine with the existing flavors, creating a harmonious and robust profile.

06

Final Touches:

  • Drizzle a bit more extra-virgin olive oil over the entire plate for added richness and depth of flavor.
  • Optionally, add a pinch of sea salt and a twist of freshly ground black pepper to accentuate the taste further.

07

Serve Immediately:

  • Carpaccio is best served immediately after preparation to maintain its freshness and preserve the delicate texture of the meat or fish.

Enjoy this delectable Carpaccio as an appetizer or a light, sophisticated meal that showcases the essence of Italian cuisine.This classic beef carpaccio recipe captures the essence of traditional Italian flavors, ensuring a delightful culinary experience for those who appreciate the art of fine dining.
