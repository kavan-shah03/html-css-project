Minestrone

Minestrone is a traditional Italian vegetable soup that is known for its rich flavors and hearty texture. This comforting dish is typically made with a variety of seasonal vegetables, beans, and pasta, all simmered together to create a delicious and wholesome meal. The combination of fresh ingredients and aromatic herbs makes this soup a favorite among many Italian households, especially during the colder months.

Recipe for Traditional Minestrone

Ingredients

    1. Vegetables

    • 1 large onion, diced
    • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
    • 2 medium carrots, peeled and chopped
    • 2 celery stalks, chopped
    • 1 medium zucchini, diced
    • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and cut into bite-sized pieces
    • 1 cup diced potatoes
    • 1 cup chopped tomatoes or 1 can of diced tomatoes
    • 1 cup chopped seasonal vegetables of your choice (such as bell peppers, spinach, or cabbage)

    2. Herbs and Seasonings

    • 1 tablespoon fresh basil, chopped
    • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
    • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 6 cups vegetable or chicken broth
    • 2 cups water

    3. Additional Ingredients

    • 1 cup cooked small pasta (such as ditalini, small shells, or elbow macaroni)
    • 1 can of cannellini beans or kidney beans, drained and rinsed
    • Grated Parmesan cheese, for serving (optional)

Instructions

01

Sautéed Onion and Garlic:

  • Use a large pot and set it over medium heat.
  • Heat a portion of olive oil in the pot.
  • Add the diced onion into the pot.
  • Incorporate the minced garlic into the mixture.
  • Sauté the onion and garlic until the onion turns translucent.

02

Preparing Sautéed Vegetables:

  • Begin by adding the chopped carrots, celery, zucchini, green beans, and potatoes to the pan, creating a colorful medley of vegetables that will infuse your dish with rich flavors and textures.
  • Allow the vegetables to sauté gently in the pan, ensuring that the heat is evenly distributed
  • Continue sautéing the vegetables for a few minutes, taking care to periodically stir them to prevent any uneven cooking
  • Stop sautéing when the vegetables begin to soften.

03

Adding Seasonal Vegetables to Tomato Sauce:

  • Start by gently pouring the chopped tomatoes into the saucepan, allowing their rich aroma to infuse the kitchen.
  • Carefully incorporate any additional seasonal vegetables that you have carefully chosen to complement the flavors of the tomatoes, creating a vibrant and wholesome blend.
  • Stir the contents well to ensure that all the ingredients are thoroughly combined.

04

Preparing Flavorful Vegetable Soup

  • Add bay leaf, basil, parsley, dried oregano, salt, and pepper into a large pot. These flavorful herbs and spices will infuse the soup with a delicious aroma and taste
  • Next, gently stir in either vegetable or chicken broth along with water, creating a savory base
  • Bring the mixture to a boil.
  • Once the mixture reaches a boil, reduce the heat to a simmer and let the soup cook for approximately 20-25 minutes.
  • After the allotted time, check the tenderness of the vegetables to ensure they are perfectly cooked.

05

Finishing Pasta and Bean Soup:

  • Add the cooked pasta and the drained beans into the simmering soup, gently stirring it.
  • Allow the soup to simmer for another 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  • Take a moment to carefully taste the soup, paying close attention to the subtle nuances of the combined flavors. Adjust the seasonings as necessary.
  • Before serving, be sure to locate and remove the bay leaf from the soup.

06

Serving Minestrone with Freshly Grated Parmesan

  • Use a ladle to carefully transfer the Minestrone into individual serving bowls, ensuring an even distribution of the hearty soup.
  • For an added touch of flavor and richness, consider generously sprinkling each portion with freshly grated Parmesan cheese.

Enjoy your hearty and nourishing bowl of homemade Minestrone, perfect for any time of the year!
