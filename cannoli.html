Cannoli

Cannoli, a classic Italian dessert, hails from the Sicilian region and has gained international acclaim for its irresistible combination of flavors and textures. Traditionally, it consists of tube-shaped shells of fried pastry dough, filled with a sweet, creamy ricotta-based filling. The crispness of the shell contrasts with the smoothness of the filling, creating a delightful balance that captivates the palate. Often adorned with chopped pistachios, chocolate chips, or candied fruit, cannoli presents itself as a versatile dessert, offering a range of delightful variations to suit diverse tastes.

Recipe for Traditional Cannoli

Ingredients

  1. Shell Ingredients:
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour 2. 2 tablespoons granulated sugar 3. 1/4 teaspoon salt 4. 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened 5. 1 large egg, beaten 6. 1/2 cup Marsala wine 7. Vegetable oil, for frying 8. 1 large egg white, lightly beaten (for sealing)
    • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
    • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
    • 1 large egg, beaten
    • 1/2 cup Marsala wine
    • Vegetable oil, for frying
    • 1 large egg white, lightly beaten (for sealing)
  2. Filling Ingredients:
    • 2 cups whole-milk ricotta cheese, drained
    • 3/4 cup powdered sugar
    • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1/3 cup mini chocolate chips
    • 1/4 cup candied fruit or chopped pistachios for garnish (optional)

Instructions

01

Prepare the Shell Dough:

  • In a large bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt. Add the softened butter and mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  • Gradually pour in the beaten egg and Marsala wine, kneading until the dough is smooth and elastic.
  • Cover the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.

02

Shape and Fry the Shells:

  • On a floured surface, roll out the dough to about 1/8 inch thickness. Cut into 4-inch circles.
  • Carefully wrap each circle around a metal cannoli tube, sealing the edges with the egg white.
  • Heat the vegetable oil to 375°F and fry the shells until golden brown. Remove and let them cool on a paper towel-lined plate.

03

Prepare the Filling:

  • In a medium bowl, combine the drained ricotta, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth.
  • Gently fold in the mini chocolate chips.

04

Assemble the Cannoli:

  • Using a piping bag or a small spoon, fill each cooled cannoli shell with the ricotta mixture.
  • Optionally, garnish the ends of the filled cannoli with chopped pistachios or candied fruit.
  • Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld.

05

Serve and Enjoy:

  • Serve the cannoli chilled and dusted with powdered sugar for an authentic Italian dessert experience.

Mastering the art of making cannoli brings a piece of Sicily's culinary heritage to your table, delighting guests and indulging the senses with its delectable blend of textures and flavors.
